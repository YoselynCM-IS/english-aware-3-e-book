A precise (and useful) definition of sustainable energy is “sources of energy that can provide our

 

energy needs today without jeopardizing the needs (and lives) of future generations.” Renewable energy

 

sources are often considered to be sustainable as they use resources such as water, wind and sunlight

 

that are, according to some, inexhaustible. Many say that these are the only truly sustainable energy

 

sources. However, not all is as good as it sounds. These so called “renewable” sources are not able to

 

deliver reliable energy on their own. For example, hydropower, relying on water flow from a particular

 

river, may not be sustainable. Climate change may dry up rivers or change their course and leave the

 

hydro system useless.
