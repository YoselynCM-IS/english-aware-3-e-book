n the eighteenth century and the beginning of the twentieth, schools didn’t use to be like they are

 

now. They used to be one-room schools with blackboards that got this name because they used to

 

be black. Students sat in long rows, and they used to bring a present to the teacher, usually some fruit;

 

the teacher ate it during the break. Teachers didn’t use to be friendly, and used to sit down at their desk

 

to dictate the class. Students of different ages used to be in the same classroom, with only one teacher

 

teaching all the subjects. He or she used to ring a bell when it was time to come to class, to take a break

 

or to go home. Students used to tie their books with a leather strap, because there were no school bags.

 

Schools changed a lot in a hundred years!
