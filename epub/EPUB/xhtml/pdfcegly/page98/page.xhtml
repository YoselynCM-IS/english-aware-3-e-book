Compositions- Comparative

 

Comparative compositions are written to compare and contrast by using logical and formal

 

arguments, the features of two people, places or things. The purpose of this comparison is to

 

persuade your reader by saying why one object is better than the other.

 

*
Expose your topic in the first sentence.

 

*
Write about one point of view in the first paragraph.

 

*
Write about the second point of view in the second paragraph.

 

*
Finally, you can either balance both options and let your readers decide which position to

 

follow, or write in favor of the point of view you preferred and convince your reader to share

 

your point of view.
