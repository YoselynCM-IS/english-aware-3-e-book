Remember that there are four types of compositions: descriptive, narrative, expository, and

 

argumentative.

 

* An expository composition is the text where the writer explains and interprets a person, a place

 

or a thing by giving details about it. Here the writer interprets and explains the object.

 

First, you write a sentence about why you chose your topic. Explain why it interested you.

 

I decided to write about ghosts because they are interesting. Some people say they are real,

 

some other thinks it is our imagination.

 

Then, in another paragraph write all the details of your topic.

 

Some scientists believe ghosts are the energy left by people when they die. Some other

 

people think ghosts are the souls of people who died before accomplishing something. Etc.

 

Finally, end your composition with a wrapping comment. It can be a question inviting your

 

readers to investigate more or a question leaving an open ending, etc.

 

Finally, do you believe in ghosts? Do you think they are energy or sad souls?
